01 Overview

The Challenge

FashionForward is a sustainable apparel brand dedicated to minimizing its environmental footprint by reducing textile waste sent to landfills. Despite their mission, they were operating with fragmented, manual systems — causing tracking errors, slow audits, and a lack of real-time visibility across their global supply chain.

Analysts were spending 60% of their time on manual data cleaning. Tracing a single product took up to three weeks.

200+ Suppliers across 25 countries

30% Projected reduction in quality issues
02 Problem & Solution
The Problem
How might we replace FashionForward's slow, inaccurate system with a centralized dashboard that provides reliable data for both tracking and auditing?

Communication silos, lack of real-time visibility, data inconsistencies, and no centralized platform for supplier performance were creating cascading operational failures across a 25-country network.

The Solution
A unified dashboard with two core functions: reliable tracking and accelerated auditing.

Consolidate all disparate data sources into a single warehouse. Automate data ingestion to ensure real-time accuracy. Design dedicated views for live shipment tracking and instant compliance report generation — turning weeks of work into seconds.

03 UX Research

A multi-method research strategy

🗂
Stakeholder Interviews
Surfaced that analysts spent 60% of their time on manual data cleaning. Stakeholders required automated compliance alerts, streamlined onboarding, and verifiable data.
🔍
Internal Systems Analysis
Analysis of ERP, supplier, and logistics systems revealed fragmented integration and manual reconciliation — including a three-week average trace lag that underscored the need for real-time interoperability.
📡
External Research
Findings supported using IoT sensors and RFID for real-time tracking and blockchain (Hyperledger Fabric) for immutable compliance records — enhancing transparency and accuracy across the network.

04 Key Insights
01

Data silos cause cascading delays. Fragmented ERP, supplier, and logistics systems meant no single source of truth — every handoff introduced errors and lag time.

02

Auditing is manual and reactive. Compliance teams were assembling reports by hand from disconnected documents, making sustainability audits a weeks-long process instead of an instant pull.

03

Global teams lack shared context. Without a unified dashboard, cross-functional teams (logistics, procurement, sustainability) made decisions from different data snapshots, leading to misaligned priorities.

04

Supplier onboarding is a bottleneck. No centralized supplier portal meant onboarding was slow and inconsistent, creating gaps in certification tracking and performance monitoring.

05 Design Outcomes

Four dashboards, one toolkit

📊
Centralized Analytics Dashboard
The command center for supply chain managers — real-time monitoring, performance analytics, and compliance oversight in a single view.
  • Navigation panel across Logistics, Orders, Revenue, Suppliers, Customers
  • KPI tiles: total revenue, shipments, orders, delivered items
  • Data visualizations for sales trends and shipping performance
  • Shipment tracking and supplier compliance with live statuses
  • Audit and IoT risk tracking with workflow progress indicators
🏭
Warehouse Dashboard
Tracks and manages shipment data alongside live IoT sensor performance for warehouse operations teams.
  • Shipment Product List with tracking number, courier, status, transit time
  • Filter by time range, export to CSV
  • IoT device management: add/delete sensors
  • Live sensor activity trends with insight and low-stock request actions
🧵
Garment Production Dashboard
Manages clothing manufacturing orders from raw materials through finished goods with full cost and schedule visibility.
  • Work order details: product info, planned quantity, production status, costs
  • Bill of Materials tracking: fabric, thread, buttons, zippers with storage locations
  • Production operations tracker: workstations, staff assignments, progress timelines
  • Actions: purchase materials, export reports, finish production
✅
Certificate Verification Center
Independently verifies all supplier sustainability claims — ensuring certifications are authentic, accurate, and current.
  • Kanban workflow: To Do → In Progress → Completed
  • Supplier info display: certificate type, validity dates
  • Collaboration tools: comments, discussion threads, progress updates
  • Export verified certificates, generate sustainability report summaries
  • Full audit trail for accountability and traceability
End-to-end traceability of materials, components, and finished products
Real-time inventory and shipment visibility via IoT sensors and RFID
Automated compliance monitoring and certification alerts
Centralized multilingual supplier portal for onboarding and document management
Sustainability metrics and automated reporting
Secure, role-based access control built on permission blockchain
06 Reflection & Takeaways
Key Takeaway
"Even advanced systems fail without unified data standards and cross-department collaboration."
Stakeholder-centered design drives real impact
Listening to diverse roles — from logistics coordinators to sustainability officers — surfaced insights that shaped practical, role-specific solutions rather than a one-size-fits-all dashboard.
Systems thinking connects technical and business priorities
Combining IoT, blockchain, and predictive analytics required understanding how each piece affected the whole. Seeing those interdependencies strengthened my ability to balance innovation with feasibility.
Translating complexity into clarity
The core design challenge was distilling a global, multi-role supply chain into screens that felt intuitive and actionable — not overwhelming. Every feature earned its place by directly serving a stakeholder need.
